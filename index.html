The Origin

Na'eeman.

In Arabic tradition, "Na'eeman" is spoken after someone showers, gets a fresh haircut, or renews themselves. It means: may you enjoy this freshness. For centuries along the Silk Road, cardamom was the spice of hospitality — offered after meals as a gesture of care. Naiman carries that gesture forward. A pearl-sized ritual, passed between cultures, refined for the way we live now.
